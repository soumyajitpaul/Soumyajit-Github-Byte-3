Self-"AWARE" - A Comprehensive Personal Mobile-Data Analysis

This is the Mobile Byte of the 'Interactive Data Science' class.

INTRODUCTION


The motivation of this Byte is to self analyse with the datasets that we have collected through the 'AWARE' app in our mobile. Following are the instrument detailes used for this experiment.
Mobile Platform: Android version 6.0.1
Mobile Model: Alcatel
Application: Aware
Duration of Data Collection: 21 days


Data-Collection

1. The data is collected into the mobile and transfered to 'instance' created into the Google Cloud Console.
2. The instance is also connected to the aware framework dashboard, which helped in real update of data from the device.

Data-Cleaning

I had selected two table from the SQL Database to study:
1. Locations
2. Battery
Next with the raw data-set the data is trimmed in Wrangler , catering to my study

1. "timestamp" column is broken in to 'Weekdays' and 'Hours' for this study using UNIXTIMEFORMAT Function
2. Other relevant columns are adjust accordingly and saved to csv format

STUDY_3: How do I use my Phone and how it impacts my Battery?

The Image below indicates the usage volatge versus battery health and the area(curve) represents the counts/frequency of each set.

Inferences:
1. Health Unknow: The aware could not detect the voltage use for this data sets, initially I thought of cleaning this set, however it implies the efficiency of the app to relate the health status with voltage count.
2. Health Good: A voltage of '3000' is indicated as good health and has the highest frequency count. this indicates that the phone's RAM was not pushed to its limit most of the time. Certainly it means that I am not a multitasker over my handset.
3. Health Overheat: A voltage of '3200' is indicated as Overheat and has NIL frequency count. This indicates that I have never over heated my phones as I am not a heavy user.
4. Health Dead: a voltage of '2750' is indicated as dead and has 151 frequency count. This indicates that I had my phone dead atleast one time during this 21 days.
5. Health Count Cold: a voltage of below '2700' is indicated as cold and has NIL frequency count. This indicates that I regularly charge my phone.
